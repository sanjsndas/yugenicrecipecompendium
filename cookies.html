🍪 Our Golden Cookie Commitment

Just like the perfect shortbread, we use cookies to enhance your Yugenic experience. This policy explains what cookies are, which ones we use, and how you can manage your preferences while respecting your privacy.

📅 Last updated: April 25, 2026 | Cookie Policy v2.0 (Fresh from the oven)

🍰 What Are Cookies & Similar Technologies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your device (computer, tablet, or mobile) when you visit websites. They help remember your actions and preferences (like language, theme, or login details) over a period of time, so you don't have to re-enter them each time. We also use local storage and tracking pixels for analytical purposes — all in a privacy-friendly way. At Yugenic Recipe Compendium, we believe in transparency: we never use invasive tracking.

🌟 Yugenic's golden rule: Cookies should serve you — not the other way around. We use only necessary, functional, analytical, and strictly opt-in marketing cookies (which we currently don't run). Your trust is our main ingredient.

📺 Third-Party Embedded Content (Video & Social)

Some recipe pages may include video demonstrations from platforms like YouTube or Vimeo using 'privacy-enhanced mode'. If you choose to play a video, those platforms may drop cookies on your device. Similarly, if we ever include social share buttons (without tracking until clicked), those are activated only by your interaction. We advise you to read the cookie policies of those third parties (YouTube, Vimeo). You can block these third-party cookies using your browser settings or our consent manager (when applicable).

🎥 Yugenic does not embed tracking scripts automatically. Any third-party content requires your affirmative action (e.g., clicking 'play' on a video). Your privacy is not an afterthought.

🌍 Browser & Device Controls

Most modern browsers provide options to manage cookies: block third-party cookies, clear existing cookies, or enable "Do Not Track" signals. Below are links to instructions for major browsers (for reference):

  • Google Chrome – Settings → Privacy and security → Cookies and other site data.
  • Mozilla Firefox – Options → Privacy & Security → Cookies and Site Data.
  • Safari – Preferences → Privacy → Cookies and website data.
  • Microsoft Edge – Settings → Cookies and site permissions.

You can also opt-out of analytics by enabling 'Global Privacy Control' (GPC) if your browser supports it — we honor GPC signals as a request to opt-out of non-essential cookies.
